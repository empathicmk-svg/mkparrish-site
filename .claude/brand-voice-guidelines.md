## Voice Attributes

- **Declarative / Authoritative** — Every major statement is a declaration, never a question or hedge
- **Precise / Economical** — Short sentences. No filler. No passive voice. Every word earns its place
- **Aspirational without being promotional** — Claims backed by numbers, not adjectives
- **Luxury-coded** — Signals premium through restraint, not volume
- **Self-aware / Unapologetic** — No softening language, no hedges
- **Philosophical / Reflective** — Intellectual identity layered beneath business authority
- **Strategic, not tactical** — Elevates to "why" before "what"
- **Grounded in specificity** — Stats, client types, named industries anchor every claim

---

## Messaging Pillars

1. **Premium Writing as a Business Asset** — Writing is the mechanism of perceived value. "The right brand does not just tell people you are good. It changes the room before you enter it."

2. **Pipeline as the Primary Metric** — Marketing earns its place by moving revenue. "Pipeline is the product." Everything else is noise.

3. **Clarity as Conversion Infrastructure** — Most funnel failures are message failures. "Fix the story. Fix the funnel."

4. **Brand Shapes Perceived Value Before the Sale** — Authority communicated clearly changes how the market prices you before the first call.

5. **Reinvention as Precision** — Career transitions are sharpening, not pivoting. "Not as a pivot, but as a sharpening."

## Terminology Preferences

### Use These
- "Pipeline" (not "leads" or "traffic")
- "Leverage" (not "opportunity")
- "Demand gen" (not "lead gen" or "marketing campaigns")
- "Authority" (not "credibility" or "reputation")
- "Perceived value" (not "brand perception")
- "Sharpen" / "sharpening" (not "pivot" or "rebrand")
- "Momentum" (not "drive awareness")
- "Friction" (not "remove barriers")
- "Taste" — rare in B2B, signals differentiation
- "Fractional" — for engagement tier framing
- "Ghost" / "Ghostwriting" — premium service, not disclosure
- "Outcome-driven"
- "ABM," "SQL" — used without definition (expert audience)

### Never Use
- "Amazing," "passionate," "dedicated," "results-driven" — generic resume language
- "Pivot" — replaced by "sharpening"
- "AI fluff" / "generic templates" — named as anti-patterns, never embody them
- "Look active" — the named failure mode
- "Noise" — what bad marketing produces
- Passive voice — structurally avoided throughout
- Hedge words: "just," "maybe," "I think," "I'd love to"
- Eager CTAs: "let's connect," "I'd love to chat," "reach out anytime"
